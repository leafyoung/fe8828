Exploratory Data Work on Bank Dataset

In this markdown document, I am going to analyze the Bank dataset and get insights about the data. The purpose of this task is to represent the data graphically.


1. Distribution across Marital Status

Listed below is the distribution of people by their marital status. We can see that most of data is of married people.

b1<-bank %>%
  group_by(`marital`) %>%
  summarize(Count = n())
ggplot(b1, aes(marital,Count)) +
  geom_bar(stat='identity')


2. Balance Distribution of Students grouped by Housing as a factor

Listed below is the balance distribution of people whose job is listed as student. They are grouped together by whether they have housing or not.We can see that among students, those without housing generally have higher balance.

#View(bank)
b2 <- bank %>%
  filter(job == "student")
ggplot(b2,aes(age,balance,color=housing)) +
  geom_point() + 
  geom_smooth()
## `geom_smooth()` using method = 'loess' and formula 'y ~ x'


3. Education level of Different jobs across Age

Below we can see the distribution of education level of different jobs across their age.Major inference is that most of Management has tertiary level of education and admin have secondary level.

ggplot(bank) +
geom_point(aes(job, age, color = education)) +
coord_flip()

8. Summarize balance of customers with loan=yes

Here I am finding the summary of balances of those customers whose loan=yes. Most of the balance outliers are around age 50.
